* functional form and multicollinearity
use wage2, clear

ge lwage = log(wage)
regr lwage educ
*su IQ
regr lwage educ IQ

corr educ IQ


regr lwage educ

* if I generate one variable that is the linear combination of the other ones, this is not identified
ge keduc = educ * 2
regr lwage educ
regr lwage educ keduc

* this is not true for the NOT-linear combinations, e.g. squares
regr lwage exper
ge exper2 = exper^2
regr lwage exper exper2

* multicollinearity is much more common that expected, for example:
ge log_exper = log(exper)
regr lwage log_exper

ge log_exper2 = log(exper2)
regr lwage log_exper log_exper2
* note that the log(x^2) is equal to 2*log(x) that is a linear combination of log(x)

ge log_exper2_correct = log(exper)^2
regr lwage log_exper log_exper2_correct
